# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Cloud and backend recruiters, hiring managers, and technical interviewers who are scanning quickly for evidence that Berk can build and operate credible platform systems. They arrive with limited time, usually from a resume, LinkedIn profile, or GitHub link, and need to understand the strongest proof within the first 30 seconds.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to increase interview callbacks by turning Berk's projects, internships, and technical artifacts into a clear hiring narrative. Success means the visitor can quickly identify the cloud/backend focus, inspect project proof through demos, documentation, and repositories, and find a low-friction path to request the CV or make contact.

## Brand Personality

Precise, capable, calm. The interface should feel like a technical atlas: composed, evidence-led, and inspectable. It should communicate engineering judgment through real project media, measurable outcomes, and direct navigation rather than decorative spectacle.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI portfolio patterns: beige-tinted default backgrounds, decorative glass cards everywhere, repeated tiny uppercase section eyebrows, soft gradient blobs, interchangeable card grids, and vague "modern developer" copy. Avoid making the page feel like a dry resume document or an over-animated demo that distracts from proof.

## Design Principles

- Lead with inspectable evidence: projects, demos, documentation, and repositories should appear before general biography.
- Make the strongest proof obvious in one scan: OpsCommand and A.T.O.M are the primary credibility surfaces.
- Let technical materials become the visual identity: maps, terminals, infrastructure language, and real project screenshots should carry the brand.
- Keep recruiter conversion close: contact, CV request, and social links should be easy to find without feeling pushy.
- Preserve calm confidence: motion, color, and layout should clarify hierarchy instead of shouting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, focus visibility, keyboard access, and responsive readability. Respect reduced-motion preferences, keep content visible without animation, avoid color-only meaning, and preserve bilingual English/Turkish access across all user-facing copy.
